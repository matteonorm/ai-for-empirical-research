Writing is thinking, and offloading too much of it to an LLM means offloading the thinking itself. This video explores where AI assistance helps (boilerplate, first-pass documentation, structured revision plans) and where it hurts (homogenizing your voice, replacing the judgment calls that sharpen arguments). The practical workflow: build a style guide so the LLM approximates your voice, use the strategic-revision skill to turn referee reports into prioritized task lists, and ask for comments on your drafts rather than rewrites so you stay in the driver's seat.
